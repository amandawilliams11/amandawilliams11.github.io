context of consumption




Here we see Margot Robbie playing Sharon Tate in Quentin Tarantino's Once Upon a time in Hollywood. As she's dancing we get a glimpse of L'automne by Alfons Mucha. While one may not have noticed upon first glance, here is a great example of how art has no boundaries. It is easily trasmittable in todays day and age, art pieces are being flown all around the world for people to see and admire.







Here we see Beyonce and Jay-Z for their video Apeshit. Throughout the music video, they display art work around the Lourve in the intentions of displaying the significance of culture and society alongside sound and image. Using these art pieces in this way produces an increased awarness around being Black in the traditional art space, that would not have been so visibly featured otherwise.







Here we see the robbers from La Casa de Papel (Money Heist) wearing red overalls and Salvador Dali masks. Dali's face serves as a physical symbol of the show's theme, the resistance towards the system, as the robbers wear his face they are rebelling against societal norms just as Dali once did. There is a handful of motifs between the two, and by using Dali as the face of their resistance we can see the intentions of Dali's paintings. There is a clear correlation between his paintings and his political thoughts. As most Spanish artists rejected modern capitalist society.
